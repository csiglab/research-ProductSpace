Phase I

Definition & Ontology

Establish immediate conceptual clarity. No context, no history. Pure function.

1. Product Definition (The Axiomatic Base)

1
Technical Essence

A vacuum-chambered optical projection system utilizing extreme ultraviolet radiation at 13.5nm wavelength, generated by plasma-excited tin droplets, to pattern sub-7nm features on photoresist-coated silicon wafers through reflective multilayer mirror optics.

2
Functional Role

Functioning as the bottleneck node for advanced semiconductor manufacturing — without which, no sub-7nm logic chip can be produced at scale.

3
System Position

Classification: Capital Good — Strategic Bottleneck

2. Technical Core (The Physics of Production)

Materials Basis

  • ▸ Ultra-pure tin (Sn) for plasma generation
  • ▸ Molybdenum-silicon multilayer mirrors (40+ bilayers)
  • ▸ Molecular-contaminant-free vacuum environment

Transformation Logic

1 Tin droplet generation (50,000/sec)
2 CO₂ laser vaporization
3 HARD STEP: Plasma → EUV emission
4 Mirror collection & wafer projection

Failure Modes

  • ▸ Mirror contamination from tin debris
  • ▸ EUV source power instability
  • ▸ Sub-nanometer positioning drift

Phase V

The Verdict

Strategic characterization and final synthesis.

Typology Classification
Strategic Bottleneck
High concentration + Low substitutability + Critical downstream dependency
Binding Constraint

Knowledge. Not capital, not materials. The integration of 100,000+ components into a coherent system requires tacit knowledge accumulated over decades.

Systemic Fragility

Immediate halt. If ASML stopped shipping tomorrow, advanced chip production would cease within months. No buffer, no alternative.

Value Migration

Upstream. Value is consolidating at the constraint point. ASML's margins expand as nodes shrink.

Final Output Requirement
"Who captures the value in this chain, and why?"

ASML captures the value because they hold the knowledge. Not the patents — those can be licensed or invented around. Not the capital — sovereign wealth funds have more. They hold the integration knowledge: the invisible understanding of how to make 100,000 components work together at atomic precision. This knowledge cannot be bought, stolen, or reverse-engineered. It must be built, step by step, failure by failure, over decades. And that is why this monopoly is durable.
