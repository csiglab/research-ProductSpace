PHASE I

Definition & Ontology

Product Definition
Technical Essence
Extreme ultraviolet (EUV) photolithography system utilizing 13.5nm wavelength light to pattern features below 10nm on silicon wafers, enabling the production of advanced logic and memory semiconductors through controlled photo-chemical reactions in photoresist materials.
Functional Role
Critical capital equipment in semiconductor fabrication, serving as the primary patterning engine for leading-edge logic processes (5nm, 3nm, 2nm nodes). Acts as the enabler for transistor density scaling and represents a mandatory step in producing modern CPU, GPU, and AI accelerator chips.
System Position
Downstream of wafer fabrication equipment and upstream of semiconductor design ecosystems. Functions as a systemic bottleneck in the semiconductor supply chain, with no viable substitute for EUV at sub-7nm nodes. Controls access to advanced process node technology.

Industrial Architecture
Producer Map
ASML (Netherlands) — Sole global supplier of EUV lithography systems. Manufacturing concentrated in Veldhoven, Netherlands with limited localization. Zeiss (Germany) supplies optics. Cymer (US, acquired by ASML) manufactures light sources.

Export Controlled
Advanced EUV systems restricted under Wassenaar Arrangement. China fabs (SMIC) unable to acquire EUV. This creates bifurcation in global semiconductor capability and accelerates domestic alternative development.
